Made in China

  • HBuilderX

    • HBuilderX is a free code editor from DCloud, a Chinese company. HBuilderX offers built-in grammar check. It's designed for cross-platform application development with a strong support for Vue.JS, an open-source framework authored by a Chinese developer. HBuilderX has detailed documentation, a robust developer community and a rich library of plugins. Whilst it's Made in and for China, many of its resources are available in English and other languages as well. Try it out to support Made in China!

  • PaddlePaddle

    • PaddlePaddle is a deep learning framework from China's Baidu, Inc. The framework is capable of training high- performance AI models to solves a range of issues from seed planting to defect detection. It is widely used in China and across the world by multinational companies. The framework is second only to PyTorch in terms of the number of pull requests on GitHub and Gitee.

  • MegEngine

    • MegEngine is another deep learning framework from a Chinese company. Megvii is a Chinese AI company that specialises in facial recognition. The firm was sanctioned by the United States due to its extrodinary capabilities that can pose "national security risks" to the United States's edge in AI research.

Data science

  • Simply Statistics

    • We are three biostatistics professors (Jeff Leek, Roger Peng, and Rafa Irizarry) who are fired up about the new era where data are abundant and statisticians are scientists. The views represented here are our own and do not represent the views of Johns Hopkins University, Harvard University or Dana Farber Cancer Institute.

    We’ll be posting ideas we find interesting, contributing to discussion of science/popular writing, linking to articles that inspire us, and sharing advice with up-and-coming statisticians.

  • R Bloggers

    • R-Bloggers is about empowering bloggers to empower other R users. R-Bloggers.com is a blog aggregator of content contributed by bloggers who write about R (in English). The site helps R bloggers and users to connect and follow the “R blogosphere”.

  • Towards Data Science

    • Towards Data Science Inc. is proud to run a publication where independent authors who follow our rules and guidelines can publish their work, share their knowledge and expertise, and engage a wide audience on Medium. We love connecting ideas with readers, but we're unable to vet the accuracy of every contribution — so use your best judgment and seek professional advice before taking any action based on the content on this site.

  • Statistical Odds and Ends

    • I am a data scientist with Linkedin's Data Science and Applied Research (DSAR) team. I earned my PhD in Statistics at Stanford in 2021, advised by Rob Tibshirani. I am interested in all things statistics, but especially in developing statistical software, understanding how statistical analyses can impact decision-making more effectively, as well as how statistics is communicated to the public and in the classroom.

    Short posts about concepts and ideas in statistics and related fields which interest me. This is also a place for me to clarify some of my thoughts and pen down notes for future reference.

  • Mathematical Odds and Ends

    • Short posts about concepts and ideas in mathematics (non-stats) and related fields which interest me. This is also a place for me to clarify some of my thoughts and pen down notes for future reference.

  • Beyond Solutions

    • The goal of this blog is to give you some tools to help you approach math problems in a systematic manner. We will do this by example. Each blog post will consist of one (or more) math olympiad problems, followed by my train of thought in working out the solution. The problems in this blog will range in difficulty but will generally come from competitions for grades 10-12.

  • Kenneth (Ken) Jee

    • As the Head of Data Science at Scouts Consulting Group, I spend my work days improving the performance of athletes and teams by analyzing the data collected on them. I also dabble in entrepreneurship and content creation. I'm probably best known for my YouTube channel where I (hopefully) help over 100,000 people navigate the data science landscape.

Artificial intelligence

  • The Batch

    • The Batch is an email newsletter published by Dr Andrew Ng's AI education company DeepLearningAI. This newsletter delivers the latest news, events, and analyses in AI straight to inboxes. All issues are also available on DeepLearningAI's official website.

  • Coursera

    • Coursera offers courses on AI, including those from DeepLearningAI, Dr Andrew Ng and other top researchers at top universities and companies worldwide. Most courses offered on Coursera are free and accessible. Those who pay a price would also receive certification after successfully passing courses, which they can put on LinkedIn profile and resume.

  • Baidu Brain (PaddlePaddle AI Studio)

    • Baidu is the leading Chinese-language search engine that has been investing heavily in AI technology since 2010. It is one of the pioneers in AI research and the author of the PaddlePaddle deep learning framework. The company offers AI courses on its AI Studio platform, aiming at educating billions AI practioners for the Chinese nation.

  • DeepMind Blog

    • DeepMind is a British artificial intelligence research company best known for Go. The company was acquired by Alphebat, and it continues to conduct cutting-edge research on AI. The company runs a blog in which it publishes academic papers on AI and explain how its product offerings and AI technology in general can help the world.

  • Open AI

    • Open AI is a widely successful AI research institution founded by Elon Musk and a few others. The organisation focuses on cutting-edge AI technology. Similar to DeepMind, it runs a blog where researchers at the firm publish papers and explain how AI can meaningfully transform the way we work and live.

  • Open CV

    • Open CV focuses on the computer vision branch of AI. The platform provides open-source projects around CV and offers courses and other resources for global AI researchers and developers to learn CV from experts worldwide. It is another awesome resource for AI practitioners.

  • SenseTime

    • SenseTime is a Chinese artificial intelligence software company that builds platforms capable of training AI models with high performance and low cost. The company is research-oriented. It is made up with over 70% employees being researchers who conduct research, publish papers, and open-source AI projects.

  • Scale Exchange

    • Scale AI is a leading AI company that focuses on data. The company also runs a virtual community called Scale Exchange. The Exchange hosts industry practitioners in AI to share thoughts and promote breakthroughs in AI research.

Art and design

  • Inkscape

    • Inkscape is a free and open-source software for designing clipart art. It is viewed as an equivalent of Adobe Illustrator. Artists can use this software to design a lot of objects.

  • GNU Image Manipulation Program

    • GNU Image Manipulation Program is a free and open-source software for editing photos. It is widely percieved as the free equivalent of Adobe Photoshop. Click this link to download the software to support open-source whilst fulfilling your artistic dreams. Stop aiding software piracy today!

  • Krita

    • Krita is a free and open-source software for art and design. Artists, animators and designers can use this software to do their personal work. It is highly praised in the open-source and art and design communities.

  • OpenToonz

    • OpenToonz is a free and open-source software for making 2D digital animation. It is recommended by professors and freelancers as one of the best accessible animation software if you have a low budget for your film projects.

  • Free Sketchbook from Autodesk

    • Sketchbook is drawing software from Autodesk. After it became an independent company, the software is no longer free with full feature. The link here; however, links to the original Autodesk software. You can get the last version of Sketchbook free from Autodesk's download centre.
